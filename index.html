performance proposal
"Error: Funny?" is a computational comedy performance, aiming to interrogate the ambiguity and inability of artificial intelligence simulating the utter intricacy of human mind-humor-through fabricating and delivering a stand-up comedy set procedurally. Can machines be funny if they do not make common sense? Is the attempt of computers trying to produce humor a ridiculous act itself?

The estimated time of the performance is 10 mins. The entire performance will be a generative stand-up comedy set and delivered by a robotic interface and a human performer. The bot will possess vocabularies and the speech style of one or more chosen stand-up comedians. The set material will be generated live on the spot. An alternative deliverable will be human actors performing the comedy robot's generated scripts. These actors will speak and deliver their materials based on a conductor’s signal. The topic of the script will be developed within a structured comedy writing framework. The source content will not only reflect common issues from the learning source–mainstream stand-up comedians but also pokes fun at the ambiguity of a stand-up robotic comedy performance itself. The technologies behind the scene include Markov chain, gpt-2, and other Machine learning text generation tools. The overarching concept aims to invite audiences to observe the condensed identities of the American comedy landscape through the lens of a performative machine, further provoking new thinking around our opinions towards what we truly consider humorous by watching a digital agent failing to be funny.

This performance is part of an MFA thesis investigation in "Computational Comedy" by Lan Zhang at the Design and Technology program at Parsons School of Design. Computational Comedy stands for generating comedy content using procedural methodology.
