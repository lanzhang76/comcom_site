performance proposal
"Error: Funny?" is a computational comedy performance, aiming to interrogate the ambiguity and off-limit creativity of artificial intelligence transforming and simulating the utter intricacy of human humor, through fabricating and delivering a stand-up comedy performance procedurally. Can machines be funny if they do not make sense to us? Is the attempt of computers trying to produce humor a spectacle of absurdity itself?

The estimated time of the performance is 10 mins. The entire performance will be a live-generated, gamified storytelling comedy, delivered by a robotic interface and a human performer. The two will take turns telling a story. A story conductor that instructs which agent speaks could be a potential addition to the performance. The comedy bot will use the vocabularies and the speech style of one or more chosen stand-up comedians that it has been trained on. An alternative performance will be human actors performing the comedy robot's generated scripts. These actors will speak and deliver their materials based on a conductor’s signal.

The source content will not only reflect issues and themes from the learning source, mainstream stand-up comedians’ transcripts, but also expose the perplexity of a robotic generative performance itself. Through deconstructing comic language down to algorithmic data, the program generates a reconfiguration of condensed identities of the American comedy landscape. The overarching concept aims to invite audiences to observe comedy through the lens of machine-human interaction, further provoking new thinking around our opinions towards what we truly consider humorous by watching a digital agent failing to be funny.

This performance is part of an MFA thesis investigation in "Computational Comedy" by Lan Zhang at the Design and Technology program at Parsons School of Design. Computational Comedy stands for generating comedy content using procedural methodology.
