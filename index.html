performance proposal
"Error: Funny?" is a computational comedy performance, aiming to interrogate the ambiguity and inability of artificial intelligence simulating the utter intricacy of human mind-humor-through fabricating and delivering a stand-up comedy set procedurally. Can machines be funny if they do not make common sense? Is the attempt of computers trying to produce humor a ridiculous act itself?

The estimated time of the performance is 5 mins. The entire performance will be a scripted stand-up comedy set, pre-generated, and delivered by a robotic interface. An alternative deliverable will be a human actor performing the comedy robot's script. The topic of the script will be developed within a structured comedy writing framework. The source content will not only reflect common issues from the learning sourceâ€“mainstream stand-up comedians but also pokes fun at the ambiguity of a stand-up robotic comedy performance itself. The technologies behind the scene include Machine learning and Natural language processing methods such as gpt-2, Markov chain, and other text generation tools.

This performance is part of an MFA thesis investigation in "Computational Comedy" by Lan Zhang at the Design and Technology program at Parsons School of Design. Computational Comedy stands for generating comedy content using procedural methodology. The overarching concept aims to invite audiences to observe the condensed identities of the American comedy landscape through the lens of a performative machine, further provoking new thinking around our opinions towards what we truly consider humorous by watching a digital agent failing to be funny.
